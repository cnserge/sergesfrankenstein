About the Manuscript

Draft of Frankenstein, [August or September] – [?December] 1816. | Bodleian MS. Abinger c.56 (formerly designated Dep. 477/1) consists of 62 leaves originally bound in Notebook A, one of two now disbound notebooks in which Mary W. Shelley (MWS) drafted the two-volume version of Frankenstein. The draft in c.56 runs from the beginning to the end of Volume I, concluding just as the Creature is about to begin his tale to Victor.

Original text by Mary Shelley (1816). Public domain, under a CC-BY-NC 4.0 licence.

The Authors

Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley

(1797-1851)

English novelist who wrote Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus at the age of 18. The daughter of philosopher William Godwin and feminist Mary Wollstonecraft, she created one of the most enduring works of Gothic literature.

Percy Bysshe Shelley

(1792-1822)

English Romantic poet and Mary's husband, who provided editorial assistance on Frankenstein. His contributions are visible throughout the manuscript in distinctive blue ink.
